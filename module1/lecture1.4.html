How can we design effective decision support tools that empower farmers and advisors to improve sustainability in agriculture?

In my work, I focus on this question... and we'll talk a little bit about what we've done in my lab, what tools are out there, and how you can get involved too.

Essential Challenges


  • Utility: how useful is it for users (farmers, managers, workers, etc)?

  • Usability: how easy is it to use?

  • Interoperability: can we move data across tools, devices, systems?

  • Trust: do users trust the tech with their data?

  • Resilient: are the tools robust, reliable, and adaptable? Will they stand the test of time?

So, how can we evaluate the quality of software for sustainable agriculture? I suggest starting with five characteristics of the software... first, Excel/spreadsheets have stood the test of time.

Spatio-temporal scope of decisions

Rossie et al., 2012, "Helping farmers face the increasing complexity of decision-making for crop protection", Available: https://www.jstor.org/stable/43872334

Rossi and his colleagues describe in the context of crop potection: "Strategic decisions involve one to several years both at farming and crop levels. Tactical management decisions are made day-by-day, or within a day, in response to what is happening at the crop level. Operational decisions involve quick response to unpredicted events at the crop or within-crop."

Information delivery mechanisms

Rossie et al., 2012, "Helping farmers face the increasing complexity of decision-making for crop protection", Available: https://www.jstor.org/stable/43872334

This same group of researchers have described how optimal information delivery mechanisms for different kinds of spatial scales and directionality of the information... warning services that are more along the lines of extension materials, where the information provider is pushing information to users. For example, a map displaying disease spread. IoT systems, or on-site devices, that are typically comprised of an in-field sensor and data collection tool are great for when you need to collect local data. And finally, the sweet spot for the genre of decision support tools that I work on, is really the when users want to be able to "pull" site-specific information for operational, tactical, and strategic decision making.

Case Study: Cover Crop Decision Support Tools

Soil Health Decision Space

Consider this decision space. Let's focus on one of these management practices: cover crops.

Cover Crops Services

  • Erosion protection
  • Nutrient cycling
  • Good grazing
  • Improved water quality
  • Pollinator habitats
  • Increased soil organic matter
  • ...and more.


OXFORD, MARYLAND - Aerial view of cover crops sitting next to the Chesapeake Bay © Edwin Remsberg, SARE Cover Crop Image Library

Cover crops provide a range of ecological services. Management complexity incurs a cognitive burden as you balance crop genetics, environmental conditions, cultural practices, and overall system management.
Effective Cover Cropping is Knowledge Intensive
Many tools [species, seed rate, smart mix etc] Shortcomings: not site specific, difficult to use, proprietary, siloed data
